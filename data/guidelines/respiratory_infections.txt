COMMUNITY-ACQUIRED PNEUMONIA (CAP) - SEVERITY STRATIFICATION

CURB-65 SCORING:
C = Confusion (new mental confusion): 1 point
U = Urea > 7 mmol/L: 1 point
R = Respiratory rate >= 30/min: 1 point
B = Blood pressure: systolic < 90 mmHg OR diastolic <= 60 mmHg: 1 point
65 = Age >= 65 years: 1 point

CURB-65 Severity:
Score 0-1: Low severity - consider outpatient or short-stay management
Score 2: Moderate severity - hospital admission recommended
Score 3-5: High severity - consider ICU or high-dependency care

MANDATORY CONTEXT BEFORE RECOMMENDATION:
Before recommending pneumonia treatment, ALL of the following must be confirmed:
- Age
- Weight (if dosing/CrCl calculation needed)
- Renal function or CrCl
- Penicillin allergy status (none/non-severe/severe-anaphylaxis)
- CURB-65 components (confusion, urea, respiratory rate, BP, age)
- Inpatient vs outpatient setting
- Prior antibiotic exposure and MDR risk factors where relevant
- Comorbidities

CAP EMPIRIC REGIMENS (inpatient)

CURB-65 0-1 (Low): Ambulatory or ward admission
First-line: PO Amoxicillin 1 g q8h
Alternative (penicillin allergy non-severe): PO Doxycycline 200 mg stat then 100 mg q12h
Alternative (penicillin allergy severe): PO Doxycycline 200 mg stat then 100 mg q12h OR Clarithromycin
Atypical cover: Consider adding Doxycycline or Azithromycin if atypical features.

CURB-65 2 (Moderate): Ward admission
First-line: IV Amoxicillin 1 g q8h + IV Clarithromycin 500 mg q12h
Step-down: PO Amoxicillin 1 g q8h + PO Clarithromycin 500 mg q12h
Alternative (penicillin allergy): IV Levofloxacin 500 mg q24h

CURB-65 3-5 (Severe/ICU): 
First-line: IV Co-amoxiclav 1.2 g q8h + IV Clarithromycin 500 mg q12h
Alternative (penicillin allergy): IV Levofloxacin 500 mg q12h
ICU/VAP: Discuss with ID

DOSING NOTE: Doses listed are for patients with normal renal function. Cross-check Antibiotic Renal Dose Adjustment Guidelines for CrCl < 60 mL/min, HD, PD, SLED, or CRRT.
