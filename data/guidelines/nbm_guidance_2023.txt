NBM (NIL BY MOUTH) DIABETES MANAGEMENT - CBG TARGETS AND THRESHOLDS

CBG TARGET RANGE: 4.0 - 10.0 mmol/L (INCLUSIVE on both ends)

CRITICAL THRESHOLD RULE:
Hypoglycaemia protocol: ONLY if CBG < 4.0 mmol/L (strictly less than 4.0).
CBG of exactly 4.0 mmol/L is WITHIN target range. It is NOT hypoglycaemia.
Do NOT trigger hypoglycaemia protocol for CBG >= 4.0 mmol/L.

HYPOGLYCAEMIA (CBG < 4.0 mmol/L):
Initiate hypoglycaemia protocol.

IN-TARGET (CBG 4.0 to 10.0 mmol/L inclusive):
Continue current management. Monitor per protocol.
CBG 4.0 = in-target. CBG 10.0 = in-target.

HYPERGLYCAEMIA (CBG > 10.0 mmol/L):
Initiate hyperglycaemia management per protocol.

INTERRUPTED ENTERAL FEEDS PATHWAY
If enteral feeds are interrupted after insulin has been given:
1. Use interrupted-feed pathway first.
2. Start D10% 100 mL/h.
3. Monitor CBG q1-2h as specified.
Do NOT switch to planned-NBM insulin conversion until feeds have stopped >8h or planned NBM is confirmed.

PERIOPERATIVE / PLANNED NBM MANAGEMENT
Before changing oral agents or premix insulin: identify exact NBM start/end and which meal/dose is omitted.
Resume usual glucose-lowering medication only when full diet is served unless the guideline states otherwise.

FALLING CBG (still >= 4.0):
For falling CBG that is still >= 4.0 mmol/L: intensify monitoring or escalate as per guideline.
Do NOT label as hypoglycaemia. Do NOT trigger hypoglycaemia protocol.
