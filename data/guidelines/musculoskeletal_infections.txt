TRAUMATIZED LIMB - ANTIBIOTIC SELECTION TABLE

Syndrome: Complex Trauma with No Biological Contamination (Open fracture, tendon injury, amputation - no human/animal bite, no soil)
First-line: IV Cefazolin 2 g q8h
Alternative: IV Clindamycin 600 mg q6h
Step-down: PO Cefuroxime 500 mg q12h
Notes: If slow to resolve, consider increasing Cefazolin to 2 g q6h (discuss with ID/ID pharmacist). Refer ID if slow to reverse or unusual bacteria cultured. Consider OPAT.

Syndrome: Traumatized Limb with Biological Contamination (Human or animal bites including dog bites, cat bites)
First-line: IV Amoxicillin/clavulanate 1.2 g q8h
Alternative: IV Clindamycin 600 mg q6h + IV Ciprofloxacin 400 mg q12h
Step-down: PO Amoxicillin/clavulanate 875/125 mg q12h
Notes: Tetanus prophylaxis: Tetanus vaccine IM 0.5 mL once if not done in last 5 years (Boostrix Tdap or Imovax d.T). Refer ID if slow to reverse, unusual bacteria cultured, or if bites from monkey/animal overseas.
ROUTING NOTE: Animal bites (dog, cat) and human bites route to this section (Traumatized Limb with Biological Contamination), NOT to Skin and Soft Tissue Infections.

SEPTIC ARTHRITIS

Syndrome: Septic Arthritis - Native Joint (No STD risk factors)
First-line: IV Cefazolin 2 g q8h
Alternative: IV Clindamycin 600 mg q6h
Notes: Surgical drainage is vital. Refer ID. Duration typically 4-6 weeks. Consider OPAT.

Syndrome: Septic Arthritis - Native Joint with STD risk factors (Gonococcal)
First-line: IV Ceftriaxone 2 g once daily
Notes: Surgical drainage is vital. Refer ID. Duration typically 4-6 weeks. Consider OPAT.

OSTEOMYELITIS - VERTEBRAL (Spondylodiscitis)
Notes: Refer ID. Duration typically 6 weeks. Consider OPAT.

DOSING NOTE: Doses listed are for patients with normal renal function. Refer to Antibiotic Renal Dose Adjustment Guidelines for renally-impaired patients.
